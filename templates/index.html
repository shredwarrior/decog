How scoring works

                        

                            
Bias Score

                            
Measures how much emotion, opinion, and framing bias is present.

                            Higher = too many biases
                        

                        

                            
Testability

                            
Measures how testable and explainable your claim is using razor checks.

                            Higher means stronger falsifiability and clearer empirical framing
                        

                        

                            
Logic Integrity

                            
Measures assumptions, if/then structure, contradiction control, and coherence.

                            High = well thought out; Low = illogical chain quality
                        

                        

                            
Final Score

                            
Weighted sum of Bias + Testability + Logic Integrity.

      How This App Works
      
                
            
      
            
      
                
      
                    This app analyzes your text using psychological, philosophical, and logical frameworks to evaluate argument strength.
                    It scores your argument between 0 and 100 using three dimensions: bias score, testability, and logic integrity.
                
      

                
      
                    
      What we look for
      

                    
      
                        
      Logical Fallacies
      
                        
      Flaws in reasoning structure that weaken an argument.
      
                        
      Examples: Ad Hominem (attacking the person), Strawman (misrepresenting an argument), False Dilemma (only two options presented), Appeal to Emotion.
      
                    
      

                    
      
                        
      Cognitive Biases
      
                        
      Systematic thinking shortcuts that can skew judgment without the arguer realizing it.
      
                        
      Examples: Confirmation Bias (favoring what you already believe), Anchoring Bias (over-relying on first information), Survivorship Bias (only seeing successes).
      
                    
      

                    
      
                        
      Cognitive Distortions
      
                        
      Common inaccurate thought patterns, often driven by emotion rather than evidence.
      
                        
      Examples: All-or-Nothing Thinking (black/white categories), Catastrophizing (expecting the worst), Emotional Reasoning (feelings = facts).
      
                    
      

                    
      
                        
      Philosophical Razors
      
                        
      Principles used to evaluate whether an argument is testable and epistemically sound. Passing a razor means the argument is measurable or falsifiable.
      
                        
      Examples: Occam's Razor (simplest explanation), Hitchens's Razor (evidence required for claims), Popper's Falsifiability (can it be disproven?).
      
                    
      

                    
      
                        
      Logic Integrity
      
                        
      A separate structural benchmark for explicit assumptions, if/then completeness, contradiction control, uncertainty calibration, and inferential chain stability.
      
                        
      Output style: "Biased, not testable or explainable, illogical" or "Low bias, testable and explainable, well thought out."
